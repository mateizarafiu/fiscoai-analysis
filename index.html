Option-by-Option Analysis
Financial mechanics, legal obligations, and stakeholder profiles.

Option 1 — Series B Extension

Existing investor · €10M in two tranches · 2× participating preferred · Full ratchet

Full ratchet anti-dilution

The most aggressive anti-dilution available. If FiscoAI raises a future round below €461.54/share, the Series B Extension price resets to that lower price — regardless of volume. The company must issue free shares to the investor, diluting everyone else.

Example: Series C at €300/share → Series B Extension investor's price retroactively resets from €461.54 to €300. Free shares issued at the expense of founders and early investors.

Real-World Case Studies
Each option maps to a documented transaction illustrating its structural risk in practice.
Option 1 parallel
Groupon — Series D (2010)
DST Global & Andreessen Horowitz

Groupon accepted a milestone-structured equity extension at a flat valuation with participating preferred shares. At the 2011 IPO at $20/share, layered liquidation preferences absorbed the majority of proceeds for preferred holders. Co-founder Andrew Mason's common stock received a fraction of the headline valuation.

Lesson: participating preferred at distressed valuation permanently mortgages founder upside.
Option 2 parallel
Klarna — Convertible bridge (2021–22)
Existing backers · PIK · Pre-IPO

Klarna raised convertible instruments ahead of a planned IPO with PIK interest and a conversion discount. Governance remained undisturbed. When the IPO was delayed and Klarna raised a down-round at $6.7B (vs. $45.6B peak), notes converted at discounted prices — but the company survived and filed for IPO at ~$15B in 2024.

Lesson: Option 2 works if the company reaches a qualifying event. Without one, the maturity cliff is real.
Option 3 parallel
Peloton — Debt facility (2022–23)
JP Morgan & Goldman · $750M

Peloton, pivoting from D2C to B2B while managing a cash crisis, secured a high-yield debt facility with cash maintenance covenants and IP-adjacent collateral. Annual interest competed directly with turnaround investment. Covenant breaches triggered a 2023 restructuring requiring significant equity dilution and governance concessions.

Lesson: cash-pay debt on an unprofitable pivot compounds distress. IP collateral is existential on any default.

Recommendation
Final verdict as legal and financial counsel to FiscoAI.

✓ Accept Option 2 — Convertible Debt (StarFund)

Option 2 is the only structure that simultaneously addresses FiscoAI's immediate capital needs (€10M fully committed, no milestones), preserves governance autonomy through the turnaround (no veto rights, no board seat), and maintains a viable Series C pathway.

FiscoAI's problem is not a lack of long-term potential — it is a lack of time. Option 2 solves this with the least structural damage.

Why not Option 1
  • Flat €30M pre-money signals no progress — anchors Series C at a weak baseline
  • Full ratchet anti-dilution requires a formal waiver before any Series C investor engages
  • 2× participating preference captures 90% of exit proceeds at current valuation
  • 5-category veto blocks every future financing round without Series B consent
  • Founder forfeiture clause creates legal exposure and retention risk
Why not Option 3
  • IP pledge is existential — any covenant breach gives VNTR the right to seize core technology
  • 12% cash interest (€960K–€1.44M/yr) competes directly with R&D and commercial investment
  • EBITDA breakeven within 24 months is unrealistic for a company in pivot
  • IP lien must be released before any Series C can close
  • Total cost of borrowing: ~€18.24M on a €12M facility
Two amendments to negotiate

Amendment 1 — Reduce maturity preference from 1.5× to 1× non-participating

The 1.5× participating preference at maturity conversion is disproportionate. A 1× non-participating preference is market-standard for bridge notes converting at maturity. Counter-offer: raise the maturity conversion discount slightly from 20% to 25% in exchange.

Amendment 2 — Cap the "most senior class" rights escalation

The current clause lets StarFund's converted shares automatically inherit whatever terms the Series C investor negotiates. Counter-offer: cap StarFund's accreted rights at 1× non-participating, or include a buyout option at a modest premium as part of any Series C closing.

These two amendments preserve StarFund's legitimate return for early-stage risk while removing the structural features most likely to complicate a Series C negotiation.
Final verdict

Option 2 solves FiscoAI's time problem with the least structural damage: full capital committed unconditionally, governance autonomy preserved, cap table clean for a Series C. The maturity cliff is within management's control. The governance harms of Option 1 are not. Option 2 is our recommendation.
